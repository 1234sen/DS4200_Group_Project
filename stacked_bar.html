Distribution of Obesity Levels by Transportation Method

This normalized stacked bar chart illustrates the distribution of obesity levels across different transportation methods. Each bar represents a transportation category, and segments within each bar display the proportion of individuals in each obesity category. Hover over any segment for detailed information.

Findings - Normalized Stacked Bar Chart

  • Automobile usage has a higher proportion of individuals that fall into obese categories.
  • Bike users show a healthier distribution.
  • Public transportation shows a more balanced distribution across obesity levels.
  • Walking users have the healthiest obesity distribution.

Implications:

Health Intervention Opportunities: Encouraging more active transportation to contribute to better health outcomes.

Further Research: Investigate the underlying factors that drive these trends.
