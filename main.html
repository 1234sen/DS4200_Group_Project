Obesity Level Data Visualization Analysis

Project Introduction: This project aims to analyze the relationship between obesity levels and factors such as gender, age, physical activity level, and transportation methods through data visualization. Tasks include creating heatmaps to show variable correlations, interactive bar charts to analyze obesity distribution, interactive scatter plots to explore the relationship between physical activity and obesity, and other charts to provide supplementary insights.

Data Description: The dataset contains 4 records (expandable), sourced from public obesity research data, with 17 attributes: Gender, Age, Height, Weight, family_history_with_overweight, FAVC (high-calorie food frequency), FCVC (vegetable intake frequency), NCP (main meal count), CAEC (food intake between meals), SMOKE, CH2O (water intake), SCC (calorie monitoring), FAF (physical activity level), TUE (electronic device usage time), CALC (alcohol consumption frequency), MTRANS (transportation method), NObeyesdad (obesity level).

Summary

Findings: 1) Weight and obesity level are highly correlated, and family history of overweight may also be a risk factor; 2) Individuals with higher physical activity levels tend to have lower obesity levels; 3) Transportation methods may affect obesity, with walkers having lower obesity rates.
Future Work: Increase sample size, analyze interaction effects between eating habits and obesity, and explore time-series data to observe obesity trends.
